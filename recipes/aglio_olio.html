Aglio e Olio

More Than The Sum of its Parts

Aglio e Olio is a classic Italian dish that's easy to make yet delicious and delightful. It is ready in just 20 minutes. Personally, it is one of my most favorite dishes!

Aglio e Olio means olive oil and garlic in Italian. It originated from Naples. At its core, it only really requires four ingredients, pasta, olive oil, garlic, and chili flakes. Though, of course, you can add more ingredients if you like, like parsley and parmesan.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti, linguini or penne pasta
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 head of garlic (sliced in thin strips)
  • Red chili flakes
  • Salt and pepper

Optional Ingredients

  • Olives
  • Parsley, chopped
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Lemon juice

Steps

  1. Cook the pasta in a pot of boiling water and salt for 9 minutes or according to package instructions.
  2. Meanwhile, heat the olive oil in a skillet over the lowest heat, add the garlic slices immediately and stir well.
  3. After two minutes, add the chili flakes, parsley, and olives. Turn off the heat as soon as the garlic turns golden. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Drain the cooked pasta, storing some pasta water in a cup. Immediately add the cooked pasta to the skillet and toss. Add some pasta water (about 1/2 cup) to enhance the texture.
  5. Season the pasta with salt and pepper and top it with parmesan cheese and lemon juice.
